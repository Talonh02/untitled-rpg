Honest Assessment

After reading through dozens of games, repos, and papers, here's what I actually think about your architecture. No fluff.

What's Genuinely Novel

Novel
Multi-Model NPC Tiering by Depth Score

Nobody else does this. RPGGO uses different models for different roles (narrator vs NPC vs DM), but nobody maps a continuous stat score to a model tier for individual NPCs. The idea that a washerwoman with depth 92 gets Opus while a knight with depth 28 gets Flash Lite - and the player never knows which model is running - is a genuinely new design. Watch Dogs: Legion does "uprezzing" (promoting NPCs from background to full sim), but that's a binary promotion triggered by player profiling, not a continuous stat-driven spectrum.

Novel
Persuasion via Player's Actual Words

This is the biggest gap in the entire ecosystem. Every game we researched uses stat checks for persuasion - the player clicks "Persuade [DC 15]" or accumulates approval points through scripted choices. Nobody has shipped a system where an LLM evaluates whether the player's free-text argument would actually convince this specific NPC based on their personality, values, and trust level. Facade (2005) came closest with its permissive template matching, but it mapped to authored responses, not emergent NPC behavior. Disco Elysium is the spiritual ancestor (dialogue IS the mechanic), but its checks still use dice rolls against skill thresholds. Your system - where the interpreter model evaluates argument quality against NPC psychology - would be first-of-its-kind.

Novel
Opus as Character Author (Stats-to-Soul Pipeline)

Using a frontier model to translate a stat block into a literary system prompt is new. Other systems use templates or lookup tables. The idea that Opus reads depth: 70, intelligence: 45, emotional_vulnerability: 88, education: 30 and synthesizes a qualitatively different person than depth: 68, intelligence: 72, emotional_vulnerability: 45, education: 65 is real. Nobody else is doing stat-to-prompt translation via a model.

What's Strong But Not New

Strong
"Models Speak, Python Thinks"

This is the right architecture, and you articulate it more clearly than anyone else, but RPGGO's Zagii engine already separates game logic from LLM generation. The dungeon-master-ai-project repo does the same. You're in good company, but this is an established pattern in the AI-RPG space, not an invention.

Strong
The Director (Background World Events)

One cheap model call per game-day to advance the world is a good design. But it's essentially what Stanford's Generative Agents do with their planning/reflection cycle, and what the Sims' advertisement system does for autonomous NPC behavior (without any model). What makes yours distinctive is that it's a single call that manages the entire world state, rather than per-agent planning. That's a meaningful cost optimization.

Strong
NPC Context Filtering

Giving NPCs only knowledge they'd plausibly have is the right call. Inworld AI, Convai, and Spirit AI all do this via RAG + character knowledge boundaries. Your tagged lore retrieval system is the same pattern. Not new, but essential and well-designed.

What Needs Work

Needs Work
Combat Math is Too Simple for Its Ambition

Your CPR formula is a weighted average of stats multiplied by weapon/armor factors with uniform randomness. It works, but it produces predictable outcomes. Mount & Blade uses a ^0.7 power compression exponent. Caves of Qud uses triple-singlet penetration rolls with explosion. Battle Brothers has a 4-stage damage pipeline. Your auto-resolve concept is strong (the player decides to fight, Python resolves, Sonnet narrates), but the resolution math needs more texture. Uniform random(0.8, 1.2) never produces upsets - a peasant with a lucky mace hit should occasionally drop a knight. That needs a tailed distribution.

Needs Work
No Morale / Fleeing System

Every combat system we researched - Battle Brothers, M&B, D&D, even Moldvay Basic - has morale mechanics. Enemies should sometimes flee mid-combat (ending the fight early), and your companions' courage/trust should be tested not just at combat start but during the fight. This is a big missing piece. Without it, every fight is to-the-death, which is both unrealistic and dramatically flat.

Needs Work
Mace vs Plate in the Weapon-Armor Matrix

You have mace/club vs plate at 0.85. Every historical source and every game we checked says maces were purpose-built for fighting plate armor. DF models this through material physics (blunt force transfers through rigid armor). Battle Brothers, GURPS, and historical combat manuals all agree: mace vs plate should be at or above 1.0. Your current matrix makes maces bad against plate, which contradicts 600 years of medieval warfare. Bump mace vs chain to 1.05-1.1 and mace vs plate to 0.95-1.05.

Needs Work
Relationship System Isn't Fully Designed Yet

ARCHITECTURE.md mentions "trust, suspicion, attraction, fear, debt" but MECHANICS.md doesn't have formulas for any of them. This is the deepest gap. DF uses 24 personality facets with compatibility math. CK3 uses three separate axes. CiF-CK uses non-reciprocal social networks with microtheory-based volition. You need to decide: are relationships reciprocal or non-reciprocal? How do personality traits affect compatibility? What thresholds trigger state changes (friend, enemy, lover)? How does trust decay without interaction? The combat math is 200+ lines of formulas. The relationship system needs the same treatment.

Gap
Interpreter JSON Truncation (~50% Failure)

Your playtest found the interpreter truncates JSON output ~50% of the time. Gigax solved this exact problem using Outlines for constrained structured generation - the model can only output valid JSON because the generation grammar enforces it. This is a critical infrastructure problem that will block everything else. Fix this before adding features.

Concrete Suggestions

Steal These Mechanics

Steal
1. Dwarf Fortress Compatibility Math for Relationships

DF's system is simple and generates complex behavior: 24 personality facets on 0-100 scales. Compare any two NPCs: if both score >60 or both <40 on a facet, positive compatibility. If one >60 and other <40, negative (grudge pressure). Sum the positives and negatives across all relevant facets. That's it. Apply this to your NPC relationship formation. Two NPCs in the same room will either drift toward friendship or grudge based on personality alignment. No model call needed.

compatibility = sum(+1 if aligned, -1 if opposed, 0 if neutral) across facets
Steal
2. Disco Elysium Passive Checks for NPC Internal Deliberation

When the Director needs to decide if an NPC notices something, suspects the player, or has an insight during conversation: stat + 6 vs difficulty. No dice, no randomness. If the NPC's perception is high enough, they notice. If their intelligence is high enough, they draw the right conclusion. The threshold is set by context difficulty. This is trivial to implement and replaces dozens of ad-hoc conditionals.

notice = (npc.perception + 6) >= difficulty_threshold
Steal
3. Sims Advertisement System for NPC Autonomy

NPCs deciding what to do between scenes shouldn't require a model call. Use the Sims pattern: every object and location advertises what it offers (tavern: +social, +food, -gold; temple: +wisdom, +calm; market: +gold, -time). NPC picks the option that best satisfies their current needs, weighted by personality. A hungry NPC goes to the tavern. A devout NPC goes to the temple. A greedy NPC goes to the market. All computed in Python, no LLM.

Steal
4. Stanford Generative Agents Memory Retrieval for Promoted NPCs

When an NPC gets promoted from Tier 0 to Tier 1 (player talks to them), seed their memory with their stat-block history as events. Then use the retrieval formula for choosing what they remember in conversation:

score = recency(0.995^hours) + importance(1-10) + relevance(cosine_similarity)

This means the bartender who saw you punch someone last week mentions it when you walk in, but doesn't bring up the weather from three months ago. Cheap to compute - just needs an embedding model for relevance scoring.

Steal
5. Battle Brothers / Moldvay Morale System

After the first enemy casualty, roll morale for remaining enemies: 2d6 > morale_score. Morale = courage*0.4 + loyalty*0.3 + willpower*0.3. If leader is dead: -3 penalty. If outnumbered: -2. If one enemy fails, adjacent enemies re-check at -1 (cascade). This creates rout outcomes (quick victory, less loot), heroic last stands (high-courage enemies fight to the death), and panic cascades. Three lines of code, massive dramatic variety.

Steal
6. CK3's Three-Axis Relationship Model

CK3 separates Opinion (do I like you?), Attraction (am I drawn to you?), and AI Behavioral Tendency (what kind of person am I?). Your five axes (trust, suspicion, attraction, fear, debt) are good but could be cleaner as: Trust (earned through actions, decays slowly), Attraction (personality compatibility, relatively stable), Fear (power differential, immediate), Debt (transactional, concrete). Suspicion might be better as a function of low trust + NPC personality (paranoid NPCs suspect at lower thresholds) rather than a separate tracked value.

Steal
7. Seed-Based Lazy Generation for Buildings

Derive each building's random seed from world_seed + building_id. Generate interiors on entry, discard on exit, regenerate identically on re-entry. Only promote to persistent storage when the player modifies something. This means your world can have 10,000 buildings but only store the 50 the player has actually changed.

seed = hash(f"{world_seed}:{building_id}")[:8]

Fix These Things

Fix
8. Replace Uniform Randomness with Tailed Distribution

Your random.uniform(0.8, 1.2) produces flat, predictable variance. Every fight outcome is within 20% of the calculated result. Real combat has long tails - sometimes the peasant gets lucky. Use random.triangular(0.7, 1.3, 1.0) (mode at 1.0, occasional extremes) or 1.0 + random.gauss(0, 0.12) clamped to [0.7, 1.4]. This preserves the average while allowing dramatic upsets.

Fix
9. Add Guaranteed Armor Reduction (from DCSS)

Your armor reduces severity via armor_rating / 100 as a probability modifier. DCSS adds a guaranteed damage reduction: armor always blocks at minimum armor_rating * 0.3 before any random rolls. This prevents the feel-bad of plate armor doing nothing on a bad roll. Someone in full plate should always take less damage than someone in cloth.

Fix
10. Add Power Compression Exponent (from M&B)

Your combat currently uses raw CPR ratios. M&B uses (attacker_power / defender_power)^0.7. Without compression, an elite warrior (CPR 90) vs three bandits (CPR 40 each, total 120) gets a ratio of 0.75. With compression: 0.75^0.7 = 0.82 - the elite has a better chance. But three elites (CPR 270) vs one bandit gets 6.75, and compressed: 6.75^0.7 = 4.35 - the overkill is reduced. Compression prevents both "elite slaughtered by numbers" and "numbers-advantage makes fights trivial."

Fix
11. Terrain-Parameterized Numbers Advantage

Your numbers advantage formula is fixed at 1 + log2(ratio) * 0.4. Lanchester's Laws say: in open fields, numbers advantage should be quadratic (everyone engages). In narrow passes, it should be nearly linear (only one person fights at a time). Parameterize the exponent by terrain:

open field: 0.6 | forest/hills: 0.4 | narrow/bridge: 0.15 | indoors: 0.25
Fix
12. Separate Cut vs Blunt Injury Types (from Kenshi)

Currently all injuries come from one pool regardless of weapon. Kenshi and GURPS both separate damage types with different consequences: Cutting weapons cause bleeding (worsens without treatment, potentially fatal). Blunt weapons cause stun/fracture (impairs immediately but heals on its own). This makes weapon choice matter beyond the multiplier - a mace breaks bones, a sword opens wounds. Different tactical and narrative implications.

AI RPG Codebases

The landscape of AI-powered text RPGs is thin. Most are single-model text generators (AI Dungeon pattern). Only a handful attempt what you're building.

Tier 1 - Direct Architectural Relevance

RPGGO / XTalk + Zagii Engine
github.com/RPGGO-AI/XTalk | 15 stars | TypeScript | Paper

Only production system with explicit multi-model architecture for different game roles. PMTA framework (Perception, Memory, Thinking, Action) for NPC decision-making. Centralized Message Bus for coordination. Cold-start optimization lets SOTA models pre-generate heuristics for lightweight models at runtime. Read the paper cover to cover.

Stanford Generative Agents
github.com/joonspk-research/generative_agents | 21k stars | Python

25 AI characters with memory streams (vector DB), reflection (higher-order observations), planning (daily decomposed to moment-by-moment). Memory retrieval: recency * importance * relevance. Reflection triggers when recent importance sum > 150. The foundational paper for AI NPC simulation.

Evennia (Python MUD Engine)
github.com/evennia/evennia | 2k+ stars | Python | BSD-3 | Very active

Most battle-tested Python implementation of room-based spatial worlds. Room/Exit/Object typeclass pattern. Exits as first-class objects. Every entity has one location field. Your npc.location = "tessam_ironDrum_f2_backroom" is literally Evennia's data model.

Neighborly + OrganicWorldsim
github.com/ShiJbey/neighborly | 74 stars | Python | OrganicWorldsim

Neighborly: ECS-based settlement sim with trait-based opinion system. OrganicWorldsim: Maslow hierarchy motivations, ART (Attitude/Respect/Trust) relationship edges, event-based NPC memory. Both are Python. OrganicWorldsim is the closest existing codebase to your NPC relationship design.

Combat & Stats Engines

Mount & Blade Auto-Resolve

The closest analog to your auto-resolve design. Reverse-engineered by modders.

TroopPower = (2 + Tier) * (10 + Tier) * 0.02
Damage = (0.5 + 0.5*rand) * 40 * (AttackerPower / VictimPower)^0.7 * PerkBonus

The ^0.7 exponent is key - it prevents one elite from trivially destroying 20 peasants (diminishing returns on power advantage) while still rewarding quality over quantity.

Dwarf Fortress Combat

No hit points. Every body part modeled with layered tissues. Damage depends on real physics: stress = force / contact area. Maces concentrate force (small contact area), slaps spread it. Blunt weapons are BETTER against plate, not "less bad."

Battle Brothers Damage Pipeline

1. Roll weapon damage: rand(Min, Max)
2. Armor damage = roll * ArmorMod * ForgeMod
   ForgeMod = 1 - ((helmet + body_armor) * 0.0005)  // heavier = harder to damage
3. HP damage = armor_ignore_portion + non_ignore_portion
4. Remaining armor gives 10% passive DR
Injury if: damage >= MaxHP * 0.25 (body) or 0.3125 (head)

BB's injury system is percentage-based (injury if damage > 25% of max HP). Your system uses fixed margin thresholds. Consider scaling injury severity by the damage-to-health ratio.

The Deep Systems

Dwarf Fortress Compatibility (the one to steal)

Non-reciprocal. 24 personality facets scored 0-100. Compare pairwise: both >60 or both <40 = positive. One high, one low = negative. Sum across facets = compatibility. Rank increases through idle chats on adjacent tiles. Rank 15 = friendship or grudge (based on compatibility). Rank 40 = lovers if compatible. Rank 50 = marriage. Simple math, emergent complexity.

Disco Elysium (dialogue as relationship)

Kim's trust is a hidden number built entirely from conversation choices. Modifies future skill checks when Kim is present. No gifts, no quests - just how you talk and what you do. Every dialogue choice is a potential +1 or -1 on future interactions. Your persuasion system should work like this.

CiF-CK / Comme il Faut (academic gold)

Two non-reciprocal social networks (Attraction + Friendship). Theory of Mind (beliefs about others' values, which can be wrong). Volition calculation from microtheories. Deployed as Skyrim mod with 6000+ users, 93% approval. Paper worth reading: arXiv

Firewatch (the subtle one)

10,000 tracked micro-events. No visible meters. System picks "the truest and most specific thing that can happen next." Interrupting Delilah mid-sentence is mechanically tracked and closes future paths. Proof that the best relationship tracking is invisible to the player.

Key Takeaway for Your Relationship System

Every system researched uses discrete thresholds (not gradual curves) for state changes. Every serious system tracks relationships as non-reciprocal. The best systems combine continuous scores (trust: 0-100) with discrete flags (has_saved_life, caught_lying, shared_secret). Your five axes (trust, suspicion, attraction, fear, debt) are reasonable, but you need the math. Write the formulas like you did for combat.

The Sims Advertisement System (steal this for NPC autonomy)

Every object/location "advertises" what needs it satisfies. NPC selects action by scoring all available options through personality-weighted attenuation curves. Physiological needs use exponential curves that spike when depleted. Pick randomly from top-scoring options (not absolute best - prevents robotic behavior). Zero model calls needed for NPC autonomous behavior.

Facade's NLU (relevant for your interpreter)

Permissive template matching extracts discourse acts (intent + sentiment + topic). Doesn't try to fully parse English. When it can't understand, uses deflection. Story decomposed into ~20 dramatic beats, each containing 10-100 joint dialog behaviors. You don't need perfect language understanding.

Watch Dogs: Legion "Uprezzing" = Your Tier System

Background NPCs are lightweight entities. When profiled, Census generates full identity in real-time. Promotion from background to fully-simulated is your exact Tier 0 → Tier 1 → Tier 2 pattern. Memory spreads to connected NPCs. Pre-computed daily schedules with real-time deviation (Shadows of Doubt) matches your Director's daily pass.

Procedural World Generation

The Key Insight: You Don't Need Geometry

For a text RPG, you need the graph - which rooms connect to which, what's in each room - not pixel coordinates. MUDs proved 40 years ago that rooms-as-graph-nodes with no inherent coordinates work perfectly for text games. Your spatial tree is already the right data structure.

Dwarf Fortress Worldgen Pipeline

10 phases: fractal terrain → temperature → rivers with erosion → vegetation from field interactions (biomes EMERGE from intersecting layers, not placed directly) → civilization placement → zero-player strategy game runs for N years to produce history. History comes from simulation, not authorship.
